# LegacyWiki

LegacyWiki creates a Wikipedia page for your fantasy football leagues. It uses stats to paint the picture of your team in the league's history and shows your year-by-year performance so you can talk sh*t to your friends (or figure out why you keep losing).

## Summary

I started my first fantasy football league in 2017 with a group of my friends. By 2018, we all figured out how to play fantasy football and we all started to get into it a little more. Although we were still figuring out the trade value system a little…

Halfway through the season, I started creating write-ups on the power rankings and posting it on a message board ESPN Fantasy used to provide. While I have no idea if my friends were reading it or not, I enjoyed the immersion of it.

Recently, I was perusing Wikipedia articles about my favorite sports teams and reading one of my favorite threads, The Ringer's QB Power Rankings by Steven Ruiz. I thought it was cool that there were sites with all this information.

To brainstorm, I created a Claude prompt with an initial laundry list of information I wanted the page to have. After refining the information, removing some of the "cheesier" portions that Claude wanted to include, and checking to see if Sleeper API could accommodate the requested information, I ended up with a barebones design which would serve as a great baseline (06/30/2026).
